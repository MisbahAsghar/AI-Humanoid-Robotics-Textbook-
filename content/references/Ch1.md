# Chapter 1 References: Introduction to Physical AI

## Foundational Sources (Established)

### 1. Brooks (1991) - Subsumption Architecture
**Citation**: Brooks, R. A. (1991). "Intelligence without representation." *Artificial Intelligence*, 47(1-3), 139-159.

**DOI**: [https://doi.org/10.1016/0004-3702(91)90053-M](https://doi.org/10.1016/0004-3702(91)90053-M)

**Label**: [established]

**Summary**: Seminal paper introducing subsumption architecture and arguing that intelligence emerges from layered reactive behaviors without explicit world models. Foundational work in embodied AI.

**Relevance to Chapter**: Introduced embodied AI concepts, subsumption architecture, and the idea that intelligence doesn't require internal representation.

---

### 2. Pfeifer & Bongard (2006) - Embodied Intelligence
**Citation**: Pfeifer, R., & Bongard, J. (2006). *How the Body Shapes the Way We Think: A New View of Intelligence*. MIT Press.

**ISBN**: 978-0262162395

**Label**: [established]

**Summary**: Comprehensive treatment of embodied intelligence theory, explaining how physical embodiment shapes cognitive processes and intelligence.

**Relevance to Chapter**: Core reference for embodied intelligence concepts, morphological computation, and sensor-motor loops.

---

### 3. McGeer (1990) - Passive Dynamic Walking
**Citation**: McGeer, T. (1990). "Passive dynamic walking." *The International Journal of Robotics Research*, 9(2), 62-82.

**DOI**: [https://doi.org/10.1177/027836499000900206](https://doi.org/10.1177/027836499000900206)

**Label**: [established]

**Summary**: Demonstrates morphological computation through passive dynamic walkers that achieve stable walking using leg geometry and gravity with minimal control.

**Relevance to Chapter**: Example of morphological computation principle in embodied intelligence.

---

### 4. Siciliano & Khatib (2016) - Robotics Handbook
**Citation**: Siciliano, B., & Khatib, O. (Eds.). (2016). *Springer Handbook of Robotics* (2nd ed.). Springer.

**DOI**: [https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-32552-1](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-32552-1)

**Label**: [established]

**Summary**: Comprehensive robotics reference covering kinematics, control, sensing, actuation, and planning. Standard reference for robotics fundamentals.

**Relevance to Chapter**: General robotics background, sensor characteristics, actuator limitations.

---

### 5. Thrun, Burgard & Fox (2005) - Probabilistic Robotics
**Citation**: Thrun, S., Burgard, W., & Fox, D. (2005). *Probabilistic Robotics*. MIT Press.

**ISBN**: 978-0262201629

**Label**: [established]

**Summary**: Standard textbook on probabilistic approaches to robotics, covering sensor noise models, Kalman filtering, particle filters, and SLAM.

**Relevance to Chapter**: Sensor noise models, uncertainty quantification, probabilistic state estimation.

## Citation Guidelines

**Established** sources are peer-reviewed publications or authoritative textbooks with stable citations. These form the theoretical foundation of the chapter.

**Emerging** sources are recent preprints, industry publications, or current technical reports (2024-2025) representing cutting-edge work. These are clearly dated as research and technology evolve rapidly.

**Tool documentation** refers to official software library documentation used in code examples.
